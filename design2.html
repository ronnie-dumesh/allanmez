Design II - Designing a Preschool - Spring 2018

Beginning with the diagramming and analysis of two precendents, one historical (Yaodong Sunken Courthouse), and one contemporary (Chichu Art Museum), an understanding of how both deal with orientation and the relationship between internal and external spaces was developed. Taking these elements, a hybrid model was developed that dealt with the orientation and extrusion of planes with relation to each other, in which by moving one plane a new range of possibilities and restrictions became present with the adjacent planes. Applying a temperate climate to this model allowed for the development of a new model which varied in density, plane extrusion amount, and overall orientation. Moving forward with the temperate adaptive model and applying the program of a preschool to it, a final design was developed that was based on planes that all shift and slide along each other and leave traces of those transformations with the hanging elements, openings, and depressions that create sightlines to guide inhabitants through the project.
